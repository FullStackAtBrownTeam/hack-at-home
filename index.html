Who we are

Full Stack at Brown is a Brown University club dedicated to promoting software engineering. Our members complete pro-bono projects for other Brown University clubs and members of the Brown community.

Socially distanced hacking

Hack@Home is virtual hackathon that focuses on bringing a diverse set of student hackers, designers, and thinkers together to "hack@home" and create projects that will solve different issues brought up by the coronavirus pandemic. Hackers can work on projects in groups or independently participate in CTF and dataset challenges to win sponsored prizes. Newcomers will have an opportunity to get a taste of Brown CS through workshops, the open Brown University Minecraft campus, and a diverse array of Brown University speakers and panelists. We will also be conducting a fundraising livestream during the event to raise money for different charities and organizations.

learn

In gearing up for our big day, Hack@Home has put together a series of workshops to help you get ready to hack! Our workshops cover topics such as web development, databasing, security, and 3D modelling, and range in difficulty from beginner to intermediate. Workshops will be released in pairs on the Friday of every week, according to the attached schedule. If you have any questions about the workshops, please let us know at workshops@hackathome.org - happy hacking!

Git Workshop

Wednesday May 6, 10:00AM EST Difficulty: Advanced

GitHub Actions gives teams world-class CI capabilities, helping developers merge and deploy code many times in a single day. The silent power of GitHub Actions lies in its ability to programmatically define just about any workflow to mirror your team's processes. In this workshop, you'll learn how to:

  • Create and use multiple, customized workflows
  • Implement a unit testing framework using GitHub Actions
  • Use multiple jobs in a workflow and pass artifacts between jobs
  • Configure a repository to work in conjunction with GitHub Actions workflows and your team's workflow
